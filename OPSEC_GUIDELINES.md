# Operational Security Guidelines

Strong OPSEC is a core principle of this framework.

## Key Principles

- Operate from isolated environments (virtual machines)
- Minimize artifact creation on target systems
- Use living-off-the-land techniques where possible
- Plan cleanup and trace removal from the beginning
- Destroy or reset attacking infrastructure after operations
- Maintain separation between different engagement phases when needed

## Why OPSEC Matters

Poor operational security can lead to early detection, attribution, and reduced effectiveness of red team engagements. This framework treats OPSEC as a continuous process rather than a final step.

## Recommendations

- Document OPSEC decisions during planning
- Regularly review what artifacts might be left behind
- Use dedicated infrastructure for each major engagement when possible
- Train team members on OPSEC discipline

Good OPSEC protects both the engagement and the individuals involved.
